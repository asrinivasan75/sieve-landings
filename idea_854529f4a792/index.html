Kalshi Two-Sided Market-Making Execution Toolkit

A pre-built, Kalshi-native execution engine with inventory-aware spread widening, position skew adjustments, and time-to-resolution decay baked in — capabilities that every manual market maker needs but currently must custom-build.

The problem

Individual traders and small prop desks on Kalshi who want to market-make illiquid event contracts must either write and maintain custom Python scripts from scratch or manually quote both sides of spreads while watching screens continuously. The Kalshi REST API provides no built-in inventory controls, spread-widening logic, or shock filters — every trader reinvents the same risk management wheel. When a surprise event moves a contract sharply, manual market makers are exposed to toxic flow with no automated out.

What we're building

A pre-built, Kalshi-native execution engine with inventory-aware spread widening, position skew adjustments, and time-to-resolution decay baked in — capabilities that every manual market maker needs but currently must custom-build. The switch trigger is eliminating screen-watching: a user defines their contract universe and risk parameters once, then the engine auto-quotes without supervision. No Kalshi-specific off-the-shelf equivalent exists today.
