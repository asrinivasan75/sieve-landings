Questions put directly

A funded competitor like EvenUp already does PI demand letters. What prevents them from adding NJ PIP offset logic next quarter?

Nothing technical prevents it. EvenUp has the runway to ship NJ calibration once they see traction here, and we expect they will. The relevant question is whether jurisdiction configuration is sufficient to compete — or whether the distribution relationship matters more. We already operate Casefile inside the NJ PI firms we're targeting, which means the first hundred users come from an installed base that already trusts the pipeline, not from cold acquisition. That relationship doesn't transfer when a competitor ships a checkbox. The window is measured in quarters; the kill criteria reflect that honestly.

If the generated letter contains an incorrect PIP offset figure and my client accepts an undervalued settlement, who is liable?

The attorney of record remains responsible for every figure in a letter that goes out under their signature — that is the correct answer and we will not obscure it. The product generates a review-ready draft, not a letter you send without reading. The provenance log attached to every output shows exactly which source document each figure was drawn from, making the attorney's review faster and more auditable than the current manual process. We are in active discussion with counsel regarding E&O coverage and indemnification scope, and we will not recommend the unlimited retainer tier to firms whose professional liability carriers have blanket AI-tool exclusions. That question should be answered before you sign up, not after.

What if the draft quality requires substantial rewriting — paragraphs, not just names? Doesn't that make this an expensive starting point rather than a time-saver?

Yes. If an attorney rewrites more than half the substantive paragraphs, the product has not delivered its value proposition and the economics invert. That is not a rhetorical concern — it is the third item on our internal kill criteria. The mitigation is the 50-field document graph: the letter is constructed from structured, verified fields, not prompted from a paragraph summary, which substantially reduces narrative hallucination. We are offering early access to existing Casefile users first precisely because their feedback on draft quality will determine whether we scale or rebuild before we reach cold outreach channels.
