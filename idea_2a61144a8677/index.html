Honest answers to fair questions

What happens if NJ's DORES portal blocks automated submissions?
This is the right thing to ask. We're confirming portal compatibility with NJ Division of Revenue before the beta launches — it's our first call, not an afterthought. If direct submission isn't permitted, the product generates a completed, pre-filled PDF you submit yourself through the normal portal login. That still eliminates the data re-entry work, though it removes the one-click submission. We'll be transparent about which mode is live before you pay anything.
What if my QuickBooks data isn't categorized well enough to pre-fill the ST-50 correctly?
Genuinely fair concern. ST-50 requires taxable vs. exempt sales by category, and not every QBO setup tracks this cleanly. That's exactly why the discrepancy flagging exists — any field we can't map with confidence shows a yellow flag and asks you to confirm before anything is submitted. In early testing with Shah & Sons client books, we're seeing 70–80% of fields pre-fill cleanly, with the remaining flagged for a 5-minute review. If your books are particularly sparse, the time savings shrink but the deadline reminder and PDF generation are still worth the price.
What if Intuit eventually builds this into QuickBooks directly?
Intuit could. QuickBooks Live Tax currently covers federal and state income tax — NJ compliance filings (Annual Report + ST-50) are a different workflow that's been untouched for years. If Intuit ships this natively, that would validate the problem and we'd say so plainly. In the meantime, this fills a gap that's been costing NJ small business owners money every quarter. The $49/filing price means you're not locked into anything — use it for as long as it saves you time.
