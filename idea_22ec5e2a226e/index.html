Questions worth answering directly.

This is the right question and we won't pretend the answer is simple. The QBO Transactions API surfaces most of what we need for sales classification — line items, account codes, customer records, and in most cases, resale certificate flags if they're stored in QBO. The honest uncertainty is on vendor bill detail for use-tax detection: if your out-of-state vendor invoices are booked with only a top-line amount and no item description or vendor-state field, we flag the bill for your review rather than auto-classify it. We'll tell you exactly what's auto-classified vs. needs a 30-second confirm. We are testing the API sandbox now and will publish what we find before anyone pays.

The May 2025 modernization is "Phase 1" per the NJ Division of Taxation's published roadmap. If Phase 2 introduces structured data import or direct API submission to the portal, we update the output format — the classification engine doesn't change, only the delivery format. We monitor NJ Rev. Stat. 54:32B amendments and Division guidance on a standing basis. For any filing period where a schema change affects your draft, we notify you and re-run at no charge. NJ rule updates come with advance notice; we've never seen a retroactive change to ST-50/450 line structure. The pre-fill approach remains valid today and we'll adapt if that changes.

The short answer: software that classifies transactions and populates a tax form is generally treated as a software tool, not tax preparation — the same legal framework that governs TurboTax and every payroll platform. The ST-50 draft we generate is a working document; the preparer or business owner reviews, signs, and submits. We do not submit on your behalf and we do not provide legal or tax advice. That said, we engaged NJ counsel to review the product before launch. If you're a licensed CPA or bookkeeper using this for client work, the tool functions as part of your workflow — your professional judgment and license remain the point of accountability, as they would with any software tool.
