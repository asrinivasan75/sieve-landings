The honest market map

Meshy handles game-ready mesh from photo. Tripo does multi-view reconstruction fast. Rodin targets professional topology. 3D AI Studio wraps them all in one dashboard. TRELLIS.2 is open-source, free, and genuinely impressive. The market is not empty — it's crowded with capable tools.

Where we think the gap is

Every conversation with a game dev, product designer, or digital artist turns up a specific moment of failure — hand-drawn concept art that degenerates to mush, print-ready STL that can't tolerate the topology errors these tools emit, sketch-to-rigged-character that doesn't exist anywhere. We don't know yet if any of these is a real business.

What this page does

It is a survey with a wireframe attached. No product exists. If you have hit a wall in Meshy, Polycam, or TRELLIS.2 on a specific job — not generally, but at a specific step — we want to hear that story. If nobody can tell us one, we won't build anything.

"The question isn't whether we can convert an image to a 3D model. Every tool already does that. The question is which user, at which step, in which existing workflow, hits a wall hard enough to pay for something better."

What we don't know yet

01
If Meshy, Tripo, and TRELLIS.2 already exist — why would I ever use something new?
You probably wouldn't. That's the honest answer. These tools are genuinely capable and have free tiers. The only scenario where a new entrant makes sense is if your specific workflow — a particular file format, topology constraint, or input type like hand-drawn concept art — fails repeatedly in all of them. Most workflows don't. If yours doesn't fail, this isn't for you and we'd rather you say so now than discover it after we've shipped.
02
Won't Microsoft or Meta just absorb this with TRELLIS.2 or SAM 3D Objects?
Almost certainly yes, if we build something generic. TRELLIS.2 is open-source and free as of 2025. Meta shipped SAM 3D Objects in late 2025. Any undifferentiated wrapper gets subsumed by the model providers themselves within months — we've seen this pattern play out. The only durable position is a workflow-specific tool that solves a problem the foundation model providers don't care about — usually because the addressable market is too small for them but large enough for a focused product. We don't know if that niche exists yet.
03
You haven't named a specific user or workflow — aren't you just guessing?
Yes, completely. The brief for this idea didn't name a target user, a specific failure scenario, or a credible wedge. That's not something we're hiding — it's the reason this page exists instead of a product. The open questions above come directly from our own evaluation criteria. We're publishing them because we'd rather filter for collaborators who have specific workflow failures to report than accumulate a waitlist of people interested in "image to 3D" generally. If you're here with a vague interest, the existing tools will serve you well.
