What anonymity actually means here
  • k-anonymity threshold: digest withheld if fewer than 3 observations submitted
  • No IP address or device fingerprint logged from submitters
  • Slack user IDs are hashed before storage; mapping table is never retained
  • AI synthesis layer sees only stripped text — no metadata, no user context
  • Executive account cannot issue decryption request for individual observations
  • Observation fragments in digest are paraphrased, not verbatim, when n < 5
  • Small-team mode (<8 raters): categorical patterns only, no fragments shown

Honest answers to hard questions
Will my team actually submit anything? Why would they criticize their own boss?
This is the right question, and we're not going to paper over it. Free-text, unprompted feedback on your manager is a behavior that most tools have failed to sustain. Our answer is: we're designing for the raters who already have something to say and just need a safe container. We're not designing for universal participation. The k-anonymity threshold means a digest only surfaces when signal is real — we'd rather deliver nothing than deliver noise. We'll know within weeks whether rater submission rates are viable, and we'll publish that data. If they aren't, this product doesn't work. We know that.
How can anonymity hold in a team of six? I'll know who said it.
In teams under eight, you probably will narrow it down sometimes — and we're not going to pretend otherwise. Small-team mode removes observation fragments entirely and shows only categorical patterns with no quoted language. The AI synthesis is instructed to flatten idiosyncratic phrasing. Does this eliminate inferability? No. Does it raise the bar meaningfully above "my manager can see my name attached to this response"? Yes. We also advise against deploying Blind Spot in teams smaller than five. The honest version of our anonymity guarantee: it's meaningfully stronger than anything else in your workflow right now, and it's not perfect.
Is this just a thin AI wrapper? What stops me from building this with a Google Form and GPT-4?
Not much, technically. If you have five direct reports willing to fill out a Google Form about you every week, and you're willing to run the prompt yourself, you can approximate this. What Blind Spot adds: the Slack integration that removes the Google Form step (which kills participation), the k-anonymity enforcement that a manual process can't provide, and the week-over-week pattern tracking that makes single observations meaningful in context. The synthesis layer is not magic. The infrastructure around it — submission, anonymization, threshold enforcement, longitudinal comparison — is where the product actually lives. We think that matters. You should verify that before paying for it.
