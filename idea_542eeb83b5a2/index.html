Honest answers to the hard questions

How is this different from Candor Technology or Ocrolus? They're well-funded and adjacent.›
Candor automates the underwriter's credit decision — it tells you whether to approve the loan. We stop earlier: we run the pre-underwriting triage that determines whether a file is ready for an underwriter at all. The specific gap we're targeting is configurable lender overlay rules for non-GSE products (DSCR, jumbo, non-QM) — the exact workflows where DU/LP have no opinion and BPOs are most used. Candor's public positioning is on conforming/agency products where Fannie and Freddie rules are stable. Whether that's a permanent gap or they're building toward it is a fair question — we don't have insider knowledge of their roadmap. What we can say: no current live product handles self-serve overlay configuration for non-QM products in a way that replaces a BPO workflow without PS engagement. If that changes, we'll tell you.
How long will it actually take to get through our infosec review and go live?›
We're not going to tell you "two weeks." A realistic infosec review at an IMB or credit union with a serious compliance posture takes 6–14 weeks. We have our SOC 2 audit engagement underway. We can provide a controls documentation package, a completed vendor questionnaire, and named US-based sub-processors on day one of the pilot conversation. The Encompass integration certification process is separately 6–12 months — that's an Encompass timeline, not ours, and the pilot can run on our API without LOS certification while that's in progress. We'd rather tell you this now than after you've spent 8 weeks in procurement.
What if the software costs more than our BPO when you factor in configuration and remaining human review?›
That's the kill criteria we've written into our own plan, and it's a legitimate risk. The math works at roughly 500+ loans per month if you're running DSCR and non-QM products — that's where BPO cost per file ($28–$55) exceeds our volume-tier pricing ($8–$18) by enough to absorb setup and any residual human review for edge cases the engine flags for human judgment. Below that volume, the ROI case is weaker and we'll tell you so before you sign anything. The pilot is designed to produce your actual cost-per-file number from real data, not our pro forma.
